My Favorite American Novels

These twentieth-century classics shaped the way I think about storytelling, identity, and life in the United States.

The Great Gatsby
F. Scott Fitzgerald · 1925

Fitzgerald captures the glamour and disillusionment of the Jazz Age through the tragic lens of Jay Gatsby, revealing the fragility of the American Dream.

To Kill a Mockingbird
Harper Lee · 1960

Seen through the eyes of young Scout Finch, this novel confronts racial injustice in the Deep South with warmth, humor, and moral clarity.

Invisible Man
Ralph Ellison · 1952

Ellison's layered tale follows an unnamed narrator navigating a world that refuses to see him, blending surrealism with a powerful critique of race in America.

Beloved
Toni Morrison · 1987

Morrison weaves memory, grief, and haunting into a story about the lasting scars of slavery and the fierce bonds of motherhood.

On the Road
Jack Kerouac · 1957

This beat generation touchstone follows Sal Paradise's cross-country odyssey, celebrating restless energy and the search for meaning in postwar America.
